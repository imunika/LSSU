Landscape Studies

at Syracuse University

What is a landscape? And why do landscapes matter? These fundamental questions drew together faculty members, curators, and select graduate students from ten different departments and units across Syracuse University and SUNY ESF, to exchange their ideas and research methodologies at a Landscape Studies Interdisciplinary Faculty Research Seminar organized between 2018 and 2020. Landscape Studies at Syracuse University is a research website dedicated to their research and reflections. Over the course of two years, participants presented research talks, arranged film screenings, curated exhibitions, organized symposia, and attended guest lectures. They wove their way through seminar rooms, museum collections, exhibitions, the Adirondacks, and Zoom meetings. Funded by the CUSE Grant Program at Syracuse University, the seminar also featured distinguished guest speakers Harriet Ritvo, Arthur J. Conner Professor, MIT, and Mickey Mahan, Centro bus driver, who brough their unique perspectives, one from the academic world as a pioneering scholar in animal studies and environmental history, and the other, from the world of lived landscapes shaped by more than two decades of driving a city bus. Video recordings of Ritvo and Mahan’s presentations can be found on this website.
